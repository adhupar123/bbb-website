Understanding Blood-Brain Barrier Permeability

The blood-brain barrier (BBB) is a highly selective semipermeable border that protects the brain from harmful substances while allowing essential nutrients to pass through. For drug development, understanding BBB permeability is crucial for treating neurological disorders.

Our model analyzes six key molecular descriptors that influence a drug's ability to cross the BBB:

Lipophilicity (MolLogP)

Measures compound solubility and membrane crossing ability

Molecular Weight (MolWt)

Larger molecules are less likely to diffuse across membranes

Polar Surface Area (TPSA)

Related to compound polarity, affecting membrane permeability

Formal Charge (FC)

Charged molecules have reduced membrane crossing ability

Aromatic Rings

Can impact compound membrane solubility

Heavy Atoms

Contributes to overall molecular weight

Reference: F. Meng, Y. Xi, J. Huang, and P. W. Ayers, "A curated diverse molecular database of blood-brain barrier permeability with chemical descriptors," Scientific Data, vol. 8, no. 1, p. 289, Oct. 2021.
